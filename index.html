About Me

Research Experience

I am currently researching on two topics:

  1. RNA inverse folding using Graph Neural Networks: Our team tries to design an RNA sequence that folds into a certain given secondary structure. The problem of RNA sequence design is of crucial importance in bio-engineering. The problem is also known as RNA inverse folding we have tried to achieve results via a data-driven approach. I have started this research recently as a summer intern under the supervision of Pekka Orponen. The topic involves a baseline familiarity of RNA structures and an in-depth understanding of Graph Neural Network architectures and machine learning basics.

  2. Semi-Definite Programming using a natural dynamic known as the Slime Mold: We have proposed a new algorithm inspired by a natural dynamic that is able to solve positive linear programs. I have started my research on this in 2020 under the supervision of Andreas Karrenbauer and published a paper here. The topic involves advanced linear algebra as well as a good understanding of dynamical systems and convex optimization.
